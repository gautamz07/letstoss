Historic moments

A brief overview

  • Super Bowl I (1967) The first Super Bowl, played between the Green Bay Packers and the Kansas City Chiefs, had the opening coin toss decided by NFL Commissioner Pete Rozelle using a silver dollar. This moment marked the beginning of a tradition for Super Bowl coin tosses.
  • Apollo 11 Moon Landing Site Before NASA's Apollo 11 mission, astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin needed to choose the precise location on the Moon to land their lunar module. They relied on a coin toss to decide between two candidate landing sites.
  • 2002 Winter Olympics In the 2002 Winter Olympics held in Salt Lake City, a coin toss was used to determine the winner of the men's skeleton event after the final race ended in a tie. Jim Shea Jr. won the gold medal as a result of the coin toss.
  • Political Primaries In some cases, political primaries or local elections that end in a tie or are too close to call have resorted to coin tosses to determine the winner. For example, in 2016, a coin toss was used in the Democratic Iowa Caucus to allocate delegates between Hillary Clinton and Bernie Sanders.
